This project showcases an interactive **HR Attrition Dashboard** built using **Power BI** to analyze employee attrition patterns and identify key factors influencing employee turnover.  
The dashboard enables HR teams and business leaders to make **data-driven retention decisions**.

---

## üéØ Business Objective
- Analyze employee attrition trends
- Identify high-risk departments and roles
- Understand the impact of overtime, income, and satisfaction
- Support strategic HR decision-making

## üîç Key Insights

### 1Ô∏è‚É£ Department-wise Attrition
- **Sales** department shows the highest attrition rate.
- **Human Resources** follows closely.
- **Research & Development** has comparatively lower attrition.

---

### 2Ô∏è‚É£ Job Role Analysis
- **Sales Representatives** experience the highest attrition.
- **Laboratory Technicians** and **HR roles** also show elevated attrition.
- Managerial and senior roles have better retention.

---

### 3Ô∏è‚É£ Overtime Impact
- Employees working **overtime** have significantly higher attrition.
- Overtime is a strong contributor to employee burnout.

---

### 4Ô∏è‚É£ Income Band Analysis
- Employees earning **below $3K** face the highest attrition.
- Attrition decreases as income increases.

---

### 5Ô∏è‚É£ Job Level Trend
- Highest attrition occurs at **Job Level 1**.
- Attrition decreases steadily with higher job levels.

---

### 6Ô∏è‚É£ Education Field Insights
- **Life Sciences** and **Medical** fields show higher attrition counts.
- Technical and HR-related education fields show lower attrition.

---

### 7Ô∏è‚É£ Income vs Tenure
- High attrition among employees with **low income and short tenure**.
- Long-tenured employees with higher income are more stable.

---

### 8Ô∏è‚É£ Job Satisfaction & Work-Life Balance
- Low job satisfaction combined with poor work-life balance results in high attrition.
- Employees with high satisfaction and good balance are more likely to stay.

---

## ‚úÖ Recommendations

- Reduce excessive overtime by optimizing workforce planning
- Focus on early-career employee engagement and mentorship
- Review compensation structure for lower income bands
- Introduce role-specific retention strategies for Sales and HR
- Promote work-life balance through flexible policies
- Invest in employee satisfaction and career development programs
